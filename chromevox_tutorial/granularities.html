Navigation in Detail.

ChromeVox allows you to view a particular page at different levels of detail. What you have seen so far is group level. The ChromeVox right arrow and ChromeVox left arrow shortcuts allow you to move among the levels of detail. ChromeVox right will increase the level of detail - think of it as "zooming in" to content on the page. ChromeVox left will decrease the level of detail, so you can get a broader overview of page content.

ChromeVox will announce the current detail level after you switch. The group level is the least specific level of detail. Character level is the most detailed level.

Moving to an object. Object level is a more specific level of detail than group level. At the object level, you can move within groups to view individual web objects like links, buttons, headers, and edit fields. So once you navigate to a group that you want to explore in more detail, simply press ChromeVox with right arrow to zoom ChromeVox in to specific objects inside the group.

Try selecting the second listitem object within the next group by zooming into the group with ChromeVox right arrow. Then try zooming back out by pressing ChromeVox left arrow. Try this on the following group by first moving to it in group mode with ChromeVox down.

Did you see the second listitem object? Was it orange? Keep moving around until you feel comfortable.

Once you get a feel for how object and group navigation works, you can use this to quickly move around a page. If you are not interested in any links within a group, simply move past the group to the next. However, if you are interested in a link or other object within a group, press ChromeVox right to zoom to the object detail level and explore the contents of the group.
