Jump commands.

Let's talk more about objects on the page. ChromeVox allows you to jump to the next and previous objects of certain types such as headers, links, and edit fields. This helps you locate objects of interest; for example, you can scan the links on a page to find the "contact us" link.

Jump commands are invoked by a pair of keys, which you tap one at a time while holding down the ChromeVox shortcut keys. The first key specifies the direction you wish to move on the page, and the second key specifies the object type. You specify the direction you want to jump using the N or P keys (which stand for "next" and "previous"). You then tap the additional key to specify the type of object you want to jump to. So this means that to activate a jump command, you need to hold down the ChromeVox shortcut keys, tap the direction key (either N or P), and while you're still holding down the ChromeVox keys, tap the object-specific key. Please note that you don't have to keep pressing down the direction key while you tap the object-specific key.

To jump to a link, press and hold the ChromeVox keys first. Then tap N to specify you want to move forward and tap L to jump to the next link. To move to the previous link, press ChromeVox P and then L. Try jumping between the two links in next group.
