Using Left and Right to take smaller steps.

You've already learned how to explore a page using the ChromeVox up and ChromeVox down shortcuts, and change the amount of detail you see by using ChromeVox minus and ChromeVox equals. If you think of ChromeVox up and ChromeVox down as taking big steps backwards or forwards through the page, you can think about changing the level of detail as changing the "size of the step." In this section, we're going to introduce another way to take steps through the page using ChromeVox left arrow and ChromeVox right arrow. Just like ChromeVox up and ChromeVox down, you can use ChromeVox left arrow and ChromeVox right arrow to move backwards and forwards through the page. Left moves backwards just like up does, and right moves forwards just like down does. But left and right allow you to take smaller steps forwards or backwards.

Let's try taking a small step forward to get a better idea of how this works. Use ChromeVox equals until you get to sentence level navigation. Press ChromeVox down. You just moved foward by sentence. Now, press ChromeVox right. You're now moving forward by word! Press ChromeVox left to move backward by word. If you press ChromeVox up or ChromeVox down, you'll go back to moving by sentence.

You can use this technique to take a small step ahead or behind where you are on a page to get a more detailed look. For example, if you're navigating by object using ChromeVox down and go past a section of the page that sounded interesting, you can use ChromeVox left to return to that section at the sentence level. Or if you're navigating by word and would like to check the spelling of the word you just heard, you can use ChromeVox up to move before the word and then use ChromeVox right to move through the word character-by-character. Whatever the level of detail you've currently selected, ChromeVox up and down will move by that level, while ChromeVox left and right will move at the next more detailed level.
