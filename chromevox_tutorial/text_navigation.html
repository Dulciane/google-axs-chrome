Moving around text.

Continuing with our exploration of levels of detail, we represent text units such as paragraphs, sentences, words, and characters each as an additional level of detail. Imagine that any given object contains some amount of text; you can explore its text by increasing the ChromeVox detail level and therefore "zooming in" to parts of the text.

Try exploring the text of this object by moving to a more detailed level by pressing ChromeVox right. Move back to less detailed levels with ChromeVox left arrow. ChromeVox will announce the new detail level you have set.

Once you have set your desired detail level with ChromeVox right or left, you can navigate around the page at that level of detail with ChromeVox up or down. Try moving through this text by word. Press ChromeVox right until you hear "words", then press ChromeVox down to move forward word by word.

The ChromeVox Tutorial

Feel free to change the level of detail and explore text that may have hard to catch instructions. Also, feel free to move to a less detailed level when you simply want to skim a section of text. Finally, notice that you can keep moving by any of the text increments until you reach the end of the page. This means you can pass from a heading right into a link if moving by word.
