4.3 Visuals

While the primary target audience is blind users, ChromeVox provides rich visuals that highlight its interactions with the page. These are important for developers and testers, for side-by-side use of the same computer by blind and sighted companions, and for low-vision users who need a hybrid interface.

ChromeVox adds visuals by adding new elements to the web page DOM that are rendered on top of page content by giving them a large z-index. The current visuals include:

Active Indicator
A glowing outline around the current object or text rage that ChromeVox is focused on; this is synchronized with browser focus but can also land on static content that’s not normally focusable.
Lens
A visual transcript of the text that ChromeVox spoke in a large font. This enables sighted testers and developers to quickly check accessible behavior without listening to speech (which may be cumbersome for users who aren’t used to it), while also providing a hybrid interface for low-vision users.
